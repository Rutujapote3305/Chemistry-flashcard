Estimations of entropy and Free energies

1. Estimation of Entropy ()

(a) From Statistical Mechanics (Boltzmann’s Formula)

Entropy is related to the number of microstates () by: S = k_B \ln \Omega More accessible microstates → higher entropy.

(b) Entropy of an Ideal Gas

For n moles of an ideal gas: S = S_0 + n R \ln \frac{V}{V_0}

(c) Third-Law Entropy (Absolute Entropy at 0 K)

According to the Third Law of Thermodynamics: S_{T=0} = 0 \quad \text{(for a perfect crystal)} S(T) = \int_0^T \frac{C_p}{T} dT

<(a) Gibbs Free Energy Estimation ()

From enthalpy and entropy: G = H - TS \Delta G = \Delta H - T \Delta S \Delta G^\circ = -RT \ln K

(b) Helmholtz Free Energy Estimation

(c) Free Energy from Partition Function ()

Applications

Entropy estimations help in phase transition studies and information theory. Gibbs free energy calculations determine chemical reaction feasibility. Helmholtz free energy is used in condensed matter physics.
