Crystal Field Theory and the energy level diagrams for transition metal and magnetic properties

1. Crystal Field Theory (CFT) Overview

Crystal Field Theory (CFT) explains the splitting of d-orbitals in transition metal complexes due to electrostatic interactions between the metal ion and surrounding ligands. The extent of d-orbital splitting depends on:

Geometry of the complex (octahedral, tetrahedral, square planar, etc.)

Ligand field strength (strong-field ligands cause greater splitting).

Metal oxidation state and electronic configuration.

3. Magnetic Properties of Transition Metal Complexes

High-spin complexes: More unpaired electrons → Paramagnetic (attracted to a magnetic field).

Low-spin complexes: Fewer or no unpaired electrons → Diamagnetic (repelled by a magnetic field).
