Corrosion.

1. Definition of Corrosion

Corrosion is the gradual destruction of metals due to chemical or electrochemical reactions with their environment. It leads to material degradation, affecting infrastructure, vehicles, and machinery.

2. Types of Corrosion

(a) Electrochemical Corrosion (Galvanic Corrosion)

Occurs when a metal reacts with an electrolyte, forming an electrochemical cell.

\text{Anode:} \quad Fe \rightarrow Fe^{2+} + 2e^-

\text{Cathode:} \quad O_2 + 4H^+ + 4e^- \rightarrow 2H_2O ] Iron ions react with oxygen and water to form hydrated iron oxide (Fe₂O₃·xH₂O), or rust.

(b) Dry Corrosion (Chemical Corrosion)

Occurs due to direct reaction with oxygen, sulfur, or other gases.

Example: Oxidation of Copper

2Cu + O_2 \rightarrow 2CuO

(c) Pitting Corrosion

Localized attack, forming small pits on metal surfaces.

Common in stainless steel due to chloride ions ().

(d) Stress Corrosion Cracking (SCC)

Caused by mechanical stress and corrosive environment.

Example: Cracking of metal pipelines in chemical industries.

(e) Crevice Corrosion

Occurs in narrow gaps where oxygen cannot reach, leading to localized attack.

Common in bolted joints and gaskets.

3. Factors Affecting Corrosion

Presence of Moisture & Oxygen (Accelerates rusting of iron).

pH of the Environment (Acidic conditions increase corrosion).

Temperature (Higher temperatures speed up corrosion).

Electrochemical Potential (More reactive metals corrode faster).

4. Corrosion Prevention Methods

(a) Protective Coatings

Painting, Galvanization (Zn coating), Electroplating prevent direct metal exposure to the environment.

(b) Cathodic Protection

Sacrificial Anode Method: Using a more reactive metal (e.g., Zn or Mg) to protect iron.

Zn \rightarrow Zn^{2+} + 2e^-

(c) Corrosion Inhibitors

Chemicals that reduce oxidation rates (e.g., phosphate coatings).

(d) Material Selection

Using stainless steel, alloys, or corrosion-resistant metals.

(e) Environmental Control

Removing moisture, reducing oxygen exposure, and using dehumidifiers.

5. Applications and Consequences of Corrosion

Infrastructure Damage: Bridges, pipelines, and buildings deteriorate.

Automobile Industry: Rust weakens vehicle frames.

Marine Industry: Saltwater accelerates ship corrosion.

Biomedical Implants: Corrosion of metal implants affects health.

Electrical Systems: Corrosion causes short circuits and failures.
