Equation of State of Real Gases & Critical Phenomena

1. Equation of State for Real Gases

Real gases deviate from ideal behavior due to intermolecular forces and finite molecular volume. Common equations of state include:

Van der Waals Equation:

\left( P + \frac{a}{V^2} \right) (V - b) = RT

P = Pressure

V = Molar volume

T = Temperature

R = Gas constant

and

a and b = Van der Waals constants (accounting for intermolecular forces and volume)

Other Equations of State:

Redlich-Kwong equation

Peng-Robinson equation

Berthelot and Dieterici equations

2. Critical Phenomena

Critical Temperature (): The temperature above which a gas cannot be liquefied by pressure alone.

Critical Pressure (): The minimum pressure required to liquefy a gas at its critical temperature.

Critical Volume (): The volume occupied by one mole of substance at the critical state.

Law of Corresponding States: Expresses that gases behave similarly when reduced variables (, , ) are used:

P_r = \frac{P}{P_c}, \quad V_r = \frac{V}{V_c}, \quad T_r.
