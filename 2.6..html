Application.

1. Identification of Molecules

IR spectroscopy is widely used to identify functional groups in organic and inorganic compounds.

Microwave spectroscopy helps determine molecular structures by analyzing rotational transitions.

2. Environmental Monitoring

Detects pollutants (e.g., CO, NO₂, CH₄) in the atmosphere.

Monitors greenhouse gases (CO₂, H₂O, O₃) using remote sensing techniques.

3. Astrophysics and Space Research

Helps identify interstellar molecules based on their spectral fingerprints.

Used in telescopes like JWST to analyze exoplanet atmospheres.

4. Medicine and Biochemistry

FTIR spectroscopy analyzes biomolecules, proteins, and lipids.

Used in breath analysis to detect diseases (e.g., diabetes, lung infections).

5. Forensic Science

IR spectroscopy detects drug compounds, explosives, and trace evidence.

Microwave spectroscopy helps analyze chemical residues at crime scenes.

6. Material Science and Nanotechnology

Characterizes nanomaterials, polymers, and thin films using IR absorption spectra.

Determines crystallinity and defects in solid-state materials.

7. Industrial Applications

Quality control in pharmaceuticals, food, and petroleum industries.

Detects contaminants in food products via vibrational spectroscopy.
