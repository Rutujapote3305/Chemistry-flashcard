Potential Energy Surfaces (PES) of H₃, H₂F, and HCN – Short Notes

1. H₃ (Trihydrogen system)

Simplest three-body system, crucial for understanding reaction dynamics.

PES shows a symmetric, multi-minima structure due to identical H atoms.

Studied in the context of H + H₂ reactions and Jahn-Teller distortions.

Important in astrophysics and plasma chemistry.

2. H₂F (Hydrogen Fluoride system)

Involves a strong polar bond (H-F) and weak H-H interactions.

PES features a deep minimum corresponding to stable HF + H and transition states for dissociation.

Key system in studying proton transfer and hydrogen bond formation.

Relevant in atmospheric and combustion chemistry.

3. HCN (Hydrogen Cyanide system)

Exhibits isomerization between HCN and HNC.

PES shows a deep minimum for HCN, with a shallower one for HNC.

Transition states govern interconversion and dissociation to H + CN.

Important in interstellar chemistry and prebiotic molecular formation.
