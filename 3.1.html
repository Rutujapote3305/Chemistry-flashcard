Ionic, Dipolar, and Van der Waals Interactions – Engineering Chemistry Notes

1. Ionic Interactions:

Definition: Strong electrostatic forces between oppositely charged ions.

Example: NaCl (Sodium chloride), where Na⁺ and Cl⁻ are held by ionic bonds.

Properties: High melting/boiling points, soluble in water, conduct electricity in molten/aqueous states.

2. Dipolar Interactions:

Definition: Attractions between molecules with permanent dipoles (partial positive and negative charges).

Types:

Dipole-Dipole: Between two polar molecules (e.g., HCl).

Hydrogen Bonding: Strong dipole interaction involving H bonded to N, O, or F (e.g., H₂O, NH₃).

Properties: Moderate strength, influences boiling points and solubility.

3. Van der Waals Interactions:

Definition: Weak intermolecular forces arising due to temporary or induced dipoles.

Types:

London Dispersion Forces: Weakest, present in all molecules, stronger in larger molecules.

Dipole-Induced Dipole: Occurs when a polar molecule induces a dipole in a nonpolar molecule.

Properties: Influence physical states of gases and liquids, weaker than ionic/dipolar forces.

These interactions are crucial in determining the physical and chemical properties of materials.
