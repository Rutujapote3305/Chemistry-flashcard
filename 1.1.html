Schrodinger wave equation

The Schrödinger Wave Equation is a fundamental equation in quantum mechanics that describes how the quantum state of a system evolves over time. It is a partial differential equation that governs the behavior of quantum particles, such as electrons, in terms of a wave function ψ (psi).

Time-Dependent Schrödinger Equation (TDSE):

iħ(∂/∂t)ψ = Ĥψ

i= imaginary unit ()

ħ= reduced Planck's constant ()

ψ= wave function

t = time

Ĥ= Hamiltonian operator (total energy operator)

Time-Independent Schrödinger Equation (TISE):

For systems with a time-independent potential, the equation simplifies to:

Ĥψ = Eψ

is the total energy of the system.

Significance of Schrödinger's Equation:

It determines allowed energy levels of quantum systems (e.g., electrons in atoms). It explains wave-particle duality by describing particles as wave functions. It predicts probabilistic behavior of particles, rather than deterministic paths.
